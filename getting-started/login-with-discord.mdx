Ruger Bot uses Discord OAuth to authenticate you, so there's no separate username or password to manage. When you click **Login with Discord**, you're taken directly to Discord's authorization screen where you review and approve the permissions Ruger Bot needs. Once you authorize, Discord redirects you back to your Ruger Bot dashboard — populated with every server where you have the **Manage Server** permission.

## Signing In

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to rugerbotapp.com">
    Open [rugerbotapp.com](https://rugerbotapp.com) in your browser. On the homepage, click the **Login with Discord** button. You'll be redirected to Discord's official OAuth authorization page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the requested permissions">
    Discord displays a list of permissions Ruger Bot is requesting on your behalf. Read through these carefully before proceeding — see the [Requested Permissions](#what-ruger-bot-requests) section below for a full breakdown of what each permission is used for.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Authorize">
    Once you're comfortable with the listed permissions, click the **Authorize** button on the Discord screen. Discord verifies your identity and generates a secure access token for Ruger Bot.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Land on your Ruger Bot dashboard">
    Discord redirects you back to Ruger Bot, where your dashboard loads automatically. You'll see a list of every Discord server you manage — click any of them to open that server's control panel.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What Ruger Bot Requests

Ruger Bot asks for the minimum permissions needed to identify you and list your servers. No message content or private data is ever accessed.

<Accordion title="Read your username and avatar">
  Ruger Bot reads your Discord display name and profile picture to personalize your dashboard header and activity log entries. This data is never shared or sold.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Read your server list">
  To populate your server selector, Ruger Bot fetches the list of Discord servers your account belongs to. Only servers where you also hold **Manage Server** appear in your Ruger Bot dashboard.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Read Manage Server permission status">
  Ruger Bot checks each server in your list for the **Manage Server** permission flag. This ensures that only authorized administrators can access and modify a server's dashboard configuration.
</Accordion>

<Note>
  Only servers where your Discord account holds the **Manage Server** permission are shown in your Ruger Bot dashboard. If a server you own isn't appearing, confirm that your role in that server includes **Manage Server** — even server owners should verify this if they use a custom role setup.
</Note>

<Tip>
  You can revoke Ruger Bot's OAuth access at any time. In Discord, go to **User Settings → Authorized Apps**, find Ruger Bot in the list, and click **Deauthorize**. Revoking access immediately signs you out of the Ruger Bot dashboard and removes the stored access token.
</Tip>
